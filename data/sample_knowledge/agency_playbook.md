# Agency Playbook

## Core offer

LeadWin Agency helps B2B companies build predictable outbound sales pipelines. The agency combines ideal customer profile research, account-list building, buying-trigger research, personalized cold email, LinkedIn touchpoints, and weekly campaign optimization.

## Best-fit clients

The best clients sell high-ticket B2B services or software, have a defined market, and can handle additional sales calls. Strong fits include SaaS companies, cybersecurity vendors, managed service providers, consulting firms, logistics providers, and specialist professional services.

## Common pain points

Prospects usually ask for help when inbound is inconsistent, paid ads are expensive, referrals are unpredictable, or sales teams waste time on unqualified meetings. The strongest buying signals are a need for qualified leads, sales-qualified opportunities, booked demos, pipeline growth, and better conversion from outbound campaigns.

## Delivery process

The first week focuses on offer clarity, ICP definition, lead-source selection, and campaign messaging. The agency then builds segmented account lists, writes personalized outreach, launches controlled experiments, and reports on replies, positive replies, meetings booked, show rates, SQLs, and pipeline value.

## Proof points

A cybersecurity SaaS client used a trigger-based outbound campaign to book 31 qualified demos in 90 days while reducing poor-fit meetings. A managed services provider used industry-specific messaging to generate 18 sales-qualified opportunities from mid-market manufacturing accounts. A consulting firm repositioned its offer around cost reduction and won 4 enterprise discovery calls in the first month.

## Objection handling

If a prospect says they already tried outbound, ask about list quality, trigger relevance, message personalization, deliverability, and follow-up discipline. If they worry about brand risk, propose a narrow pilot with approved messaging, small send volume, and weekly quality review. If they ask for guarantees, anchor the conversation around leading indicators and pipeline economics rather than vanity metrics.
